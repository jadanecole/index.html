My Favorite Dog

The Doberman Pinscher is widely recognized as one of the most intelligent and agile breeds. These dogs are all muscle and full of energy. No matter the hour, Dobermans are ready for action; whether that action is training, running, or guarding.

They come in a few different shades:
  • black and rust
  • red and rust
  • blue and rust
  • fawn

My Drakey is red, the superior choice (no bias here).



Known for their eagerness to learn and easy ability to train, they make well-rounded family dogs. Their unique build and intense appearance have often given them the reputation for being intimidating or even dangerous. However, any Dobie owner will tell you the honest truth; which is that behind that serious look is a loyal, affectionate, drama-queen who just wants attention and will betray your trust for snacks.

A Brief History of the Doberman

The Doberman Pinscher was created in the late 19th century by a tax collector called Karl Friedrich Dobermann, who wanted a protective companion to accompany him throughout his workday. By selectively breeding dogs known for strength, speed, and trainability, he created a breed which quickly became of value to many different lines of work such as police work and military services. Due to this very history, Dobermans are often associated with sharp instincts, fearlessness and protectiveness, making them the exceptional working dogs.
